The Definitive Guide to 15 V60 Pour-Over Masters and Their Exact Recipes

Across the modern specialty coffee world, a small group of brewers, educators, competitors, and innovators have shaped how the V60 is brewed today. While their philosophies differ — some favor agitation, others reject it entirely — a surprising convergence exists in their core parameters:

• Ratios cluster between 1:15 and 1:17
• Water temperatures are typically 92–100°C
• Bloom phases range between 2×–3.5× dose
• Final brew times target 2:30–4:30
• Even extraction is universally diagnosed by a flat bed

This document consolidates the brewing approaches of 15 influential figures and translates their grind philosophies into Fellow Ode Gen 2 equivalents.

---

Fellow Ode Gen 2 Reference

Approximate range: ~275–1,160 microns (community-measured; Fellow does not publish official micron specs)
Dial range: 1–11 (31 total steps, ~3 clicks per numbered position)

NOTE: All Ode Gen 2 grind mappings below are community estimates. None come from the experts themselves.

General mapping (from Fellow hopper lid guide + community consensus):

Ode 2 1–3 → Prismo / AeroPress (espresso-style)
Ode 2 3–6 → AeroPress (standard)
Ode 2 4–8 → Pour-over (V60, Chemex) — start at 5 for V60
Ode 2 6–7 → Cupping
Ode 2 7–9 → Batch brew / electric drip
Ode 2 8–10 → French Press
Ode 2 9–11 → Cold Brew

Target drawdown across most methods: 2:30–3:30.

1. James Hoffmann — The Structured Even Extraction Method

Dose: 30g
Water: 500g
Ratio: 1:16.7
Temperature: "The hotter, the better" — just off boil for light roasts; Hario Europe page lists 97°C
Grind: Medium-fine (Ode 2: ~4–5)

Bloom:
60g water (2× dose, up to 3× if needed)
45 seconds
Swirl brewer to saturate

Pour 1:
0:45–1:15
Vigorous spiral from center outward to 300g

Pour 2:
1:15–1:45
Gentle center pour to 500g

Stir once clockwise and counterclockwise
Final swirl

Drawdown: ~3:30

Iced version (verified):

Dose: 32.5g
Hot water: 300g
Ice in server: 200g
Temp: 95°C
Grind: Ode 2: ~3.5–4.5 (finer than hot)

Bloom: 65g
Pour remainder at 0:45
Total brew: ~2:55

Note: 32.5g dose maintains ~1:15.4 ratio against 500g total liquid (300g water + 200g ice).

2. Tetsu Kasuya — 4:6 Method (2016 World Brewers Cup Champion)

Dose: 20g
Water: 300g
Ratio: 1:15
Temperature: 92°C (competition parameter; Philocoffea recommends ~93°C for light roasts)
Grind: Coarse (Ode 2: ~5.5–6.5)

Competition pour structure (unequal):

50g (0:00)
70g (0:45)
60g (1:30)
60g (2:10)
60g (2:40)

Simplified version: 5 × 60g equal pours.

Each begins when bed runs dry.
No swirl or stir.

Total time: ~3:30

Alternative Ice Brew (verified — Kurasu Kyoto / Kasuya's YouTube):

Dose: 50g
Ice placed directly on bed: 500g
Cold water: 100g

Slow melt extraction over 4–15 hours. Ratio: 1:12.
Note: Kasuya describes this as experimental — "lacks reproducibility." A second variant uses 300g ice + 300g cold water (same 1:12 ratio).

3. Scott Rao — Two-Pour V60 Method (verified via Hario UK)

Dose: 20g
Water: 330g
Ratio: ~1:16.5
Temp: 97°C (kettle temperature; his broader philosophy is "as hot as possible")
Grind: Ode 2: ~4–5

Bloom:
60g
Aggressive spin

After 40s:
Pour to 200g
Gentle spin

When ~70% drained (his exact wording):
Pour to 330g
Gentle spin

Total time: 4:00–4:30

Note: This is his two-pour method for home use. He also has a faster one-pour café method (under 2:15). Recommends plastic V60 for heat retention. Targets 22–24.5% extraction.

4. Matt Winton — Five-Pour Method (2021 World Brewers Cup Champion)

Dose: 20g
Water: 300g
Ratio: 1:15
Temp: 93°C bloom, ~88°C for later pours (dual-kettle system)
Grind: Coarse (Ode 2: ~5–6)

Five pours of 60g each
Begin next pour when bed nearly dry
No stirring; relies on aggressive pour height for agitation

Total time: ~3:30 (home adaptation; competition routine may run longer)

Note: Previously listed as "Pulse Turbulence Method" — this name does not appear in any verified source. Consistently called the "Five-Pour Method" or "5-Pour V60."

5. Emi Fukahori — Hybrid Immersion Method (2018 World Brewers Cup Champion)

Dose: 17g
Water: 220g
Ratio: ~1:12.9
Grind: Coarse
Brewer: GINA by Goat Story (V60-shaped cone with valve — NOT a standard Hario V60)

Two-temperature extraction:

Stage 1 (0:00–0:45): 50g at 80°C, valve closed (immersion)
Stage 2 (0:45–1:45): 100g at 95°C, valve open (drip)
Stage 3 (1:45–2:30): 70g at 80°C, valve closed (immersion)
Stage 4 (2:30–2:55): Drain (drawdown)

Total: ~2:55

Note: Low ratio justified by Laurina varietal (naturally low caffeine, resistant to over-extraction). First woman to win WBrC. Method has been widely adapted to the Hario Switch.

6. Patrik Rolf — April Coffee V60 Method (2019 WBrC Silver Medalist)

Dose: 20g
Water: 300g
Ratio: 1:15
Temp: 92°C
Grind: Coarse (Ode 2: ~5.5–6.5)

Six pours:

50g × 6 (at 0:00, 0:40, 1:10, 1:40, 2:10, 2:40)

All aggressive circular pours

~3:20–3:30

Source: aprilcoffeeroasters.com blog, "How we Brew Coffee with a V60 Filter Brewer" (August 2018), authored by Patrik Rolf Karlsson.

Note: Previously listed as "Multi-Pour Turbulence" — this name does not appear in any verified source. The recipe has no branded name.

7. Lance Hedrick — Versatile V60 Recipe

Dose: 20g
Water: 340g
Ratio: 1:17
Temp: 100°C
Grind: Medium, ~720 microns (Ode 2: ~4–5)

Pour 1 (bloom):
50g at 0:00, swirl

Pour 2 (second bloom):
50g at 0:30, swirl

Main pour 1:
120g at 1:00, swirl

Main pour 2:
120g at 1:30, swirl

~2:30–3:30 (target ~3:00)

Note: Previously listed as "Double Bloom" — that name belongs to a different recipe by Standout Coffee (Lukas/@nordkaffeeguy). Hedrick calls this his "Ultimate Pourover Recipe" or "V60 Brew: A Versatile Recipe for Any Coffee."

Iced adaptation (verified — "Ultimate Flash Brew" YouTube video):

Dose: 20g
Brew water: 240g
Ice added after: 60g (8:2 water-to-ice ratio)
Pour schedule: bloom 60g (wait 1 min), pour 90g (at 1:00), pour 90g (at 1:45)
Stir ice into brew until cooled to 5–15°C, then serve over fresh ice.

8. Jonathan Gagné — V60 Recipe (astrophysicist, author of "The Physics of Filter Coffee")

Dose: 22g
Water: 374g
Ratio: 1:17
Temp: 100°C
Grind: Ode 2: ~4–5

Bloom:
77g (3.5× dose)

Two pours after bloom (to 200g, then to 374g)
Spin after each

Total: ~4:03

Source: coffeeadastra.com, "A V60 Pour Over Video" (June 2019). Credits Scott Rao's method as primary influence.

Note: Previously listed as "Physics-Based Method" — he does not name his method.

9. Tim Wendelboe — Staged Pour Method (2004 World Barista Champion)

Published recipe (timwendelboe.no):

Dose: 32.5g (scaled as 65g per liter)
Water: 500g
Ratio: 1:15.4 (65g/L)
Temp: Not published on his website
Grind: Ode 2: ~3.5–5 (adjust by taste)

Bloom: ~60g, stir to saturate, 30 seconds
Pour to 200g
Continue in circular motions to 500g

Total: ~3:00–3:30

Note: Previously described as "Continuous Pour" — his published method actually uses staged pours (bloom → pour to 200g → continue to 500g), not a single unbroken pour. "Stir during bloom" is confirmed. Temperature of 96–100°C appears in community sources but is not on his official website.

10. Ben Put — Monogram Coffee V60 Recipe (7× Canadian Barista Champion)

Dose: 21g
Water: 340g
Ratio: 1:16
Temp: 93°C (200°F)
Grind: Medium-coarse (Ode 2: ~4.5–5.5)

Bloom: 50g, 30 seconds

Main pour to 250g
Final pour to 340g

~2:30–3:00

Source: monogramcoffee.com/blogs/news/brew-series-pourover (July 2021, authored by Jill Hoff). This is the Monogram Coffee house recipe; Ben Put is co-founder.

Shared Consensus

• Ratios: 1:15–1:17
• Temperature: near boiling
• Flat bed = even extraction

Major Divides

• Agitation vs none
• Fine vs coarse grind
• Few pours vs pulse pours

Dial-in always depends on taste outcome.
