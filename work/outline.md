## I. Introduction
- 15 influential V60 brewers profiled (originally 17; David Beaman and Chumpon Poolsawat removed — unverifiable identities)
- 11 have published/verified recipes; 4 have no V60 recipe
- Core parameter convergence across methods:
  - Ratios: 1:15–1:17 (exception: Fukahori at ~1:12.9)
  - Temperatures: 92–100°C
  - Bloom: 2×–3.5× dose
  - Total brew time: 2:30–4:30
  - Flat bed = even extraction (universal diagnostic)

## III. Expert Profiles & Recipes

### 1. James Hoffmann — Structured Even Extraction ✅ VERIFIED
- **Hot:** 30g / 500g, 1:16.7, "as hot as possible" (Hario page: 97°C), Ode ~4–5
  - Bloom 60g (2× dose) / 45s with swirl
  - Pour to 300g (0:45–1:15, vigorous spiral from center outward)
  - Pour to 500g (1:15–1:45, gentle center)
  - Stir 1× CW + CCW, final swirl
  - Drawdown ~3:30
- **Iced:** 32.5g / 300g hot + 200g ice, 95°C, Ode ~3.5–4.5 — **VERIFIED**
  - Bloom 65g, pour remainder at 0:45, ~2:55
  - 32.5g maintains ~1:15.4 ratio against 500g total liquid

### 2. Tetsu Kasuya — 4:6 Method (WBrC 2016 Champion) ✅
- **Standard:** 20g / 300g, 1:15, 92°C (competition) / ~93°C (light roast general), Ode ~5.5–6.5
  - Competition pours: 50/70/60/60/60 (unequal). Simplified: 5 × 60g.
  - Each when bed runs dry. No swirl or stir. ~3:30
- **Ice Brew:** 50g dose, 500g ice on bed, 100g cold water — **VERIFIED**
  - 1:12 ratio. Slow melt 4–15 hours. Kasuya calls it experimental.

### 3. Scott Rao — Two-Pour V60 Method ✅ (verified via Hario UK)
- 20g / 330g, ~1:16.5, 97°C (kettle temp), Ode ~4–5
- Bloom 60g + aggressive spin
- Pour to 200g + gentle spin (after 40s)
- Pour to 330g + gentle spin (when "70% drained" — his exact wording)
- Total: 4:00–4:30 (two-pour method; one-pour café version under 2:15)

### 4. Matt Winton — Five-Pour Method (WBrC 2021 Champion) ✅
- 20g / 300g, 1:15, **93°C bloom / ~88°C later pours (dual-kettle)**, Ode ~5–6
- 5 × 60g, next when bed nearly dry
- No stirring; uses aggressive pour height for agitation
- ~3:30 (home version)
- ~~"Pulse Turbulence Method"~~ → name not found in any source

### 5. Emi Fukahori — Hybrid Immersion Method (WBrC 2018 Champion) ✅
- 17g / 220g, ~1:12.9, **GINA brewer** (not standard V60)
- Stage 1: 50g at 80°C, valve closed (immersion) — 0:00–0:45
- Stage 2: 100g at 95°C, valve open (drip) — 0:45–1:45
- Stage 3: 70g at 80°C, valve closed (immersion) — 1:45–2:30
- Stage 4: drain — 2:30–2:55
- Low ratio justified by Laurina varietal. First woman to win WBrC.

### 6. Patrik Rolf — April Coffee V60 Method (WBrC 2019 Silver) ✅
- 20g / 300g, 1:15, 92°C, Ode ~5.5–6.5
- 6 × 50g (at 0:00, 0:40, 1:10, 1:40, 2:10, 2:40), aggressive circular
- ~3:20–3:30
- Source: aprilcoffeeroasters.com blog (Aug 2018)
- ~~"Multi-Pour Turbulence"~~ → name not found in any source

### 7. Lance Hedrick — Versatile V60 Recipe ✅ VERIFIED
- **Hot:** 20g / 340g, 1:17, 100°C, Ode ~4–5
  - 50g (0:00) → 50g (0:30) → 120g (1:00) → 120g (1:30), swirl after each, ~3:00
  - ~~"Double Bloom"~~ → name belongs to Standout Coffee, not Hedrick
- **Iced ("Ultimate Flash Brew"):** 20g / 240g + 60g ice — **VERIFIED**
  - 8:2 water:ice ratio. Pour schedule: bloom 60g, 90g, 90g.

### 8. Jonathan Gagné — V60 Recipe (astrophysicist, coffeeadastra.com) ✅
- 22g / 374g, 1:17, 100°C, Ode ~4–5
- Bloom 77g (3.5× dose), 2 pours + spin after each, ~4:03
- Source: coffeeadastra.com (June 2019). Credits Rao's influence.
- ~~"Physics-Based Method"~~ → he doesn't name his method

### 9. Tim Wendelboe — Staged Pour Method (WBC 2004 Champion) ⚠️ CORRECTED
- Published recipe (timwendelboe.no): 32.5g / 500g, ratio 1:15.4 (65g/L)
- Bloom ~60g + stir, 30s → pour to 200g → continue to 500g
- ~3:00–3:30
- ~~"Continuous Pour"~~ → actually staged pours
- Temperature NOT published on his website (community says ~98°C)

### 10. Ben Put — Monogram Coffee V60 Recipe (7× Canadian Barista Champion) ✅
- 21g / 340g, 1:16, 93°C (200°F), Ode ~4.5–5.5
- Bloom 50g / 30s → pour to 250g → pour to 340g
- ~2:30–3:00
- Source: Monogram Coffee blog (July 2021, by Jill Hoff — house recipe)

## IV. Iced Brewing Styles (General)
- **Traditional flash brew:** 60% hot water / 40% ice
- **Post-brew dilution:** Full hot extraction, ice added after
- **Ice-on-bed:** Slow melt (Kasuya variant, verified)

## V. Consensus & Divides
### Shared
- Ratios: 1:15–1:17
- Temperature: near boiling preferred
- Flat bed = even extraction

### Divides
- Agitation vs. none
- Fine vs. coarse grind
- Few pours vs. pulse pours
- Dial-in always depends on taste
