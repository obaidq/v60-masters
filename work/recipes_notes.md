## Per-Recipe Notes

### James Hoffmann — Hot ✅
- Well-documented (YouTube). Bloom time (45s) explicitly stated.
- Ode setting adjusted from 3.5–4.5 to ~4–5 per community consensus (Fellow starting point = 5).

### James Hoffmann — Iced ✅ VERIFIED
- All parameters confirmed against multiple sources reproducing his YouTube video.
- 32.5g dose is deliberate: maintains ~1:15.4 ratio against 500g total liquid (300+200).

### Tetsu Kasuya — 4:6 ✅ VERIFIED
- Competition used unequal pours: 50/70/60/60/60. The 5×60g is the simplified teaching version.
- Temperature: 92°C = competition. 93°C = Philocoffea light-roast recommendation. Both legitimate.
- Ode range narrowed from 5.5–7 to ~5.5–6.5 (7 is likely too coarse for V60).

### Tetsu Kasuya — Ice Brew ✅ VERIFIED
- Real published method. Two variants exist: (A) 500g ice/100g water, (B) 300g ice/300g water. Both 1:12.
- Kasuya himself says it "lacks reproducibility" — experimental/fun, not professional technique.
- Source: Kurasu Kyoto blog + Kasuya's YouTube.

### Scott Rao ✅ VERIFIED
- All parameters confirmed from Hario UK ambassador interview.
- "~70% drained" is his exact wording, not an approximation.
- 97°C is the recipe-specific temp; his philosophy is "as hot as possible" to compensate for heat loss.
- This is his two-pour home method. One-pour café method is under 2:15.

### Matt Winton ✅ VERIFIED (with corrections)
- Name: "Five-Pour Method" — "Pulse Turbulence" not found in any source.
- **Dual-kettle system:** 93°C for bloom, ~88°C for later pours. Draft had flat 93°C.
- Brew time: 3:30 is the home target. One source reports competition at ~6:50 (disputed).
- WBrC 2021 Champion, representing Switzerland (Australian by birth).

### Emi Fukahori ✅ VERIFIED
- All parameters (17g/220g, 80°C/95°C switching, 3 stages + drain) confirmed exactly.
- **Brewer: GINA by Goat Story**, not a Hario V60. Has valve for immersion/drip switching.
- Low ratio (~1:12.9) justified by Laurina varietal (low caffeine, resistant to over-extraction).
- WBrC 2018 Champion, first woman to win.

### Patrik Rolf ✅ VERIFIED
- All parameters confirmed from April Coffee blog (Aug 2018, authored by Patrik Rolf Karlsson).
- "Multi-Pour Turbulence" not found in any source. Recipe has no branded name.
- Pour schedule confirmed: 0:00, 0:40, 1:10, 1:40, 2:10, 2:40.
- WBrC 2019 silver medalist; founder of April Coffee, Copenhagen.

### Lance Hedrick — Hot ✅
- Parameters from draft not yet individually verified against video but consistent with known sources.
- bloom_g = 100 (50+50 combined double bloom).

### Lance Hedrick — Iced ✅ VERIFIED
- 20g/240g/60g ice confirmed. Now includes full pour schedule: bloom 60g, 2×90g.
- Uses 8:2 water:ice ratio (not the traditional 6:4 Japanese flash brew split).
- Source: "Ultimate Flash Brew" YouTube video.

### Jonathan Gagné ✅ VERIFIED
- All parameters (22g/374g/1:17/77g bloom/2 pours/spin/~4:03) confirmed from coffeeadastra.com (June 2019).
- Correct URL: **coffeeadastra.com** (two e's in "coffee").
- He doesn't name his method. "Physics-Based" was an editorial label.
- Astrophysicist (PhD, U de Montréal). Plaskett Medal recipient. Author of "The Physics of Filter Coffee."

### Tim Wendelboe ⚠️ CORRECTED
- Published recipe: 32.5g/500g at 1:15.4 (65g/L). NOT the ranges previously listed.
- "Continuous pour" was WRONG — his method uses staged pours (bloom → 200g → 500g).
- Temperature NOT published on timwendelboe.no. Community says ~98°C.
- "Stir during bloom" confirmed.
- WBC 2004 Champion (barista, not brewers cup).

### Ben Put ✅ VERIFIED
- 21g/340g/1:16/93°C/bloom 50g/30s confirmed from Monogram Coffee blog.
- Blog authored by Jill Hoff — this is the house recipe, not personally Ben Put's.
- Ben Put: 7× Canadian Barista Champion, co-founder of Monogram Coffee.

### Elika Liftee ⚠️ RECIPE REMOVED
- Identity verified: 2× US Brewers Cup Champion (2020, 2022), WBrC 3rd (2021), WBrC 2nd (2022).
- Previously listed 15g/250g/50g bloom NOT found attributed to him. Likely confused with Onyx Kalita Wave recipe (16g/250g/50g bloom).
- His competition work used Kalita Wave, not V60.
