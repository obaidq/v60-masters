# The Definitive Guide to 15 V60 Pour-Over Masters and Their Exact Recipes

## Introduction

Across the modern specialty coffee world, a small group of brewers, educators, competitors, and innovators have shaped how the V60 is brewed today. While their philosophies differ — some favor agitation, others reject it entirely — a surprising convergence exists in their core parameters:

- Ratios cluster between 1:15 and 1:17
- Water temperatures are typically 92–100°C
- Bloom phases range between 2×–3.5× dose
- Final brew times target 2:30–4:30
- Even extraction is universally diagnosed by a flat bed

This document consolidates the verified brewing approaches of 15 influential figures. Of these, 11 have published or verifiable V60/pour-over recipes; 4 are included for their influence on the broader coffee competition landscape but have no published V60 method. All grind references include Fellow Ode Gen 2 estimates where applicable.

## 1. James Hoffmann — Structured Even Extraction

**Background:** 2007 World Barista Champion. Founder of Square Mile Coffee Roasters. Author and YouTuber with the most-viewed V60 tutorial online.

### Hot V60 (Verified)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Dose | 30g |
| Water | 500g |
| Ratio | 1:16.7 |
| Temperature | "As hot as possible" — Hario Europe page lists 97°C |
| Grind | Medium-fine (Ode 2: ~4–5) |
| Bloom | 60g (2× dose), 45s, swirl brewer |
| Pour 1 | Vigorous spiral from center outward to 300g (0:45–1:15) |
| Pour 2 | Gentle center pour to 500g (1:15–1:45) |
| Agitation | Stir 1× clockwise + 1× counterclockwise, then final swirl |
| Total time | ~3:30 |

### Iced V60 (Verified)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Dose | 32.5g |
| Hot water | 300g |
| Ice in server | 200g |
| Temperature | 95°C |
| Grind | Finer than hot (Ode 2: ~3.5–4.5) |
| Bloom | 65g, pour remainder at 0:45 |
| Total time | ~2:55 |

The 32.5g dose maintains a ~1:15.4 ratio against the full 500g of liquid (300g water + 200g ice).

## 2. Tetsu Kasuya — 4:6 Method

**Background:** 2016 World Brewers Cup Champion (Japan). First Asian WBrC winner. Founder of Philocoffea. Hario ambassador.

### Standard V60 (Verified)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Dose | 20g |
| Water | 300g |
| Ratio | 1:15 |
| Temperature | 92°C (competition); ~93°C (Philocoffea light-roast recommendation) |
| Grind | Coarse (Ode 2: ~5.5–6.5) |
| Pours | Competition: 50/70/60/60/60. Simplified: 5 × 60g. |
| Timing | Each pour begins when bed runs dry |
| Agitation | None |
| Total time | ~3:30 |

The first two pours (40% of water) control sweetness and acidity balance. The last three (60%) control strength.

### Ice Brew — Experimental (Verified)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Dose | 50g |
| Ice on bed | 500g (placed directly on coffee) |
| Cold water | 100g |
| Ratio | 1:12 |
| Time | 4–15 hours (slow melt) |

Kasuya describes this as experimental — "lacks reproducibility." A second variant uses 300g ice + 300g cold water (same 1:12 ratio). This is not flash brew; it is a sub-zero-temperature slow extraction.

## 3. Scott Rao — Two-Pour V60 Method

**Background:** Author of multiple coffee science books. Consultant. Influential voice on extraction theory.

### V60 Recipe (Verified — Hario UK ambassador interview)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Dose | 20g |
| Water | 330g |
| Ratio | ~1:16.5 |
| Temperature | 97°C (kettle temp; philosophy: "as hot as possible") |
| Grind | Medium-fine (Ode 2: ~4–5) |
| Bloom | 60g + aggressive spin |
| Pour 1 | To 200g + gentle spin (after 40s) |
| Pour 2 | To 330g + gentle spin (when "~70% drained" — his exact wording) |
| Total time | 4:00–4:30 |

This is his two-pour home method. He also has a faster one-pour cafe method (under 2:15 total). Recommends plastic V60 for heat retention. Targets 22–24.5% extraction.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|

| Ratio | 1:15 |

## 5. Emi Fukahori — Hybrid Immersion Method

**Background:** 2018 World Brewers Cup Champion (representing Switzerland). First woman to win the WBrC.

### Competition Recipe (Verified)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Dose | 17g |
| Water | 220g |
| Ratio | ~1:12.9 |
| Grind | Coarse |
| Brewer | **GINA by Goat Story** (V60-shaped cone with valve — not a standard Hario V60) |

| Stage | Time | Valve | Water | Temp | Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00–0:45 | Closed | 50g | 80°C | Immersion |
| 2 | 0:45–1:45 | Open | 100g | 95°C | Drip |
| 3 | 1:45–2:30 | Closed | 70g | 80°C | Immersion |
| 4 | 2:30–2:55 | Open | — | — | Drain |

Total: ~2:55. The unusually low ratio is justified by the Laurina varietal (naturally low caffeine, resistant to over-extraction). This method has been widely adapted to the Hario Switch.

## Iced Brewing Styles — Overview

| Style | Method | Typical ratio |
|---|---|---|
| **Traditional flash brew** | Brew hot water (60%) over ice (40%) in server | ~60/40 water/ice (range: 55–70% / 30–45%) |
| **Post-brew dilution** | Full hot extraction, ice added after | Varies by desired dilution |
| **Ice-on-bed** | Ice placed directly on coffee; slow melt | 1:12 (Kasuya variant) |

The 60/40 flash brew split is the most commonly cited starting point across sources, though professionals adjust based on brewer, grind, and desired strength.

## Shared Consensus

- **Ratios:** 1:15–1:17 (exception: Fukahori at ~1:12.9 with Laurina varietal)
- **Temperature:** Near boiling preferred by most
- **Flat bed** = even extraction (universally accepted diagnostic)

## Major Divides

- **Agitation:** Rao (spin), Hoffmann (stir + swirl) vs. Kasuya (none), Winton (none), Rolf (pour turbulence only)
- **Grind:** Fine (Hoffmann, Rao, Gagné: Ode ~4–5) vs. Coarse (Kasuya, Winton, Rolf: Ode ~5–6.5)
- **Pour structure:** Few large pours (Rao, Hoffmann) vs. many small pulses (Kasuya, Winton, Rolf)
- **Temperature control:** Single temp (most) vs. dual-temp (Fukahori, Winton)

Dial-in always depends on taste outcome.
